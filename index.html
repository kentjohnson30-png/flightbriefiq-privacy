Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 16, 2026

FlightBriefIQ is developed by Kent Johnson. This privacy policy describes how the app handles your information.

No Data Collection

FlightBriefIQ does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on external servers. All data you enter — including pilot profile, aircraft profiles, flight details, sleep history, and fatigue assessments — is stored locally on your device and is never sent to any server, third party, or analytics service.

HealthKit Data

FlightBriefIQ requests read access to your sleep history from Apple Health solely to predict pilot fatigue and assess fitness for flight. This sleep data is:

  • Processed entirely on your device
  • Never transmitted off your device
  • Never shared with third parties
  • Never used for advertising or marketing purposes
  • Not stored separately from Apple Health — the app reads it on demand

You may deny HealthKit access at any time in Settings > Privacy & Security > Health > FlightBriefIQ. The app will continue to function; you can enter sleep history manually.

Weather and Aviation Data

FlightBriefIQ retrieves weather data (METARs, TAFs, AIRMETs, SIGMETs, winds aloft, PIREPs) from the FAA Aviation Weather Center and NOTAM data from the FAA NOTAM API. These requests contain only airport identifiers and do not include any personal information.

Third Parties

FlightBriefIQ does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or tracking frameworks. No SDKs that collect user data are included in the app.

Data Storage and Deletion

All app data is stored locally on your device using standard iOS storage. Uninstalling FlightBriefIQ permanently deletes all stored data. There is no cloud backup, sync, or account system.

Children's Privacy

FlightBriefIQ is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect information from children.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy is updated, the revised version will be posted at this URL with an updated date. Continued use of the app constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
